# Revenue Reporting Requirements
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 2, 4, 5, and 12 of the Monetization
Compliance Rules and Sections 5 and 7 of the MultiPixel Source-Available
License 2.2 — Fortress Edition.

It defines the mandatory reporting requirements for all monetized activity
within the MultiPixel ecosystem.

These requirements apply to:

• Servers
• Mods
• Businesses
• Creators
• Integrations
• Any entity generating revenue using MultiPixel technology, assets, or branding

Failure to comply is a severe violation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — REPORTING FREQUENCY
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

All monetized entities must submit revenue reports:

• Monthly (required)  
• Quarterly (automatic summary)  
• Annually (full audit summary)  

Monthly reports are mandatory regardless of:

• revenue amount  
• activity level  
• number of users  
• whether revenue was generated that month  

If no revenue was generated, a **zero‑revenue report** must still be filed.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — REQUIRED REPORT CONTENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each revenue report must include:

### 2.1 Revenue Breakdown
• total gross revenue  
• revenue by category (donations, subscriptions, cosmetics, ads, etc.)  
• revenue by platform (Stripe, PayPal, Patreon, etc.)  
• revenue by region (if applicable)  

### 2.2 Transaction Logs
• full transaction list  
• timestamps  
• payment platform identifiers  
• transaction IDs  
• item or service purchased  
• price paid  
• currency used  

### 2.3 Monetization Method Disclosure
• all monetization methods currently active  
• any new monetization methods added  
• any monetization methods removed  
• any sponsorships or advertising deals  

### 2.4 Revenue Share Calculation
• total revenue share owed  
• calculation breakdown  
• payment confirmation (if already paid)  

### 2.5 Administrative Information
• entity name  
• owner/operator name  
• contact information  
• server or mod ID  
• approval status (if required)  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — REPORT FORMAT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reports must be submitted in one of the approved formats:

• JSON  
• CSV  
• MultiPixel Revenue Report Template (preferred)  

Reports must be:

• complete  
• accurate  
• unmodified  
• machine-readable  
• submitted through the official reporting portal  

Screenshots, PDFs, or images are not accepted as primary reports.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — RECORD RETENTION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Entities must retain:

• all revenue logs  
• all transaction logs  
• all payment processor statements  
• all sponsorship agreements  
• all advertising revenue reports  
• all monetization configuration files  

Retention period:

• minimum 12 months  
• up to 36 months for high‑risk entities  
• indefinitely for entities under investigation  

Deleting logs before the retention period is a severe violation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — PAYMENT CONFIRMATION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Entities must provide:

• proof of revenue share payments  
• payment processor receipts  
• transaction IDs  
• timestamps  
• confirmation of successful transfer  

Failure to provide proof is treated as non‑payment.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — ZERO‑REVENUE REPORTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

If no revenue was generated in a reporting period, the entity must submit:

• a zero‑revenue declaration  
• confirmation that no monetization methods were active  
• confirmation that no off‑platform payments occurred  

Zero‑revenue reports prevent:

• revenue concealment  
• off‑platform monetization  
• unreported sponsorships  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 7 — LATE REPORTING
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reports are considered late if not submitted within:

• 7 days after the end of the reporting month  

Late reporting consequences:

• first offense: warning  
• second offense: moderate violation  
• third offense: severe violation  

Entities with repeated late reports may be flagged for audit.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 8 — FALSE OR MISLEADING REPORTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following are severe violations:

• falsifying revenue  
• omitting transactions  
• hiding sponsorships  
• underreporting income  
• altering logs  
• submitting incomplete reports  
• submitting manipulated reports  

False reporting may result in:

• immediate suspension  
• permanent delisting  
• legal action  
• financial penalties  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 9 — OFF‑PLATFORM PAYMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following must be reported:

• Patreon  
• Ko-fi  
• PayPal  
• Stripe  
• CashApp  
• bank transfers  
• crypto (if used illegally)  
• barter or non-cash value exchanges  

Failure to report off‑platform payments is treated as revenue concealment.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 10 — AUDIT TRIGGER CONDITIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

An audit may be triggered if:

• reports are late  
• reports are incomplete  
• reports contain inconsistencies  
• revenue drops unexpectedly  
• revenue spikes unexpectedly  
• monetization methods change suddenly  
• user complaints are received  
• automated systems detect anomalies  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 11 — AMENDMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

This document may be updated at any time.  
All monetized entities must comply with the latest version.
