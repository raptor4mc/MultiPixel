# Monetization Audit Procedure
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 4, 5, 7, and 12 of the Monetization
Compliance Rules and Sections 5 and 7 of the MultiPixel Source-Available
License 2.2 — Fortress Edition.

It defines the official procedure used by MultiPixel to audit monetized
Servers, Mods, Businesses, and Integrations.

This procedure is mandatory. Refusal to comply is a severe violation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — PURPOSE OF MONETIZATION AUDITS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Monetization audits exist to:

• verify revenue reporting accuracy  
• confirm correct revenue share payments  
• ensure monetization methods are compliant  
• detect prohibited monetization systems  
• verify payment platform integrity  
• ensure user protection and transparency  
• prevent revenue concealment  
• enforce the MultiPixel License  

Audits protect both MultiPixel and the community.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — ENTITIES SUBJECT TO AUDIT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following may be audited at any time:

• monetized Servers  
• monetized Mods  
• monetized Businesses  
• monetized Integrations  
• any entity using MultiPixel for revenue generation  

Audits may also target:

• entities with suspicious activity  
• entities with inconsistent reports  
• entities with user complaints  
• entities flagged by automated systems  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — TYPES OF AUDITS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 3.1 Scheduled Audits
Performed periodically for approved monetized entities.

### 3.2 Random Audits
Performed without notice to ensure compliance integrity.

### 3.3 Triggered Audits
Initiated due to:

• late reports  
• missing reports  
• inconsistent revenue data  
• sudden revenue spikes or drops  
• user complaints  
• automated anomaly detection  

### 3.4 Emergency Audits
Initiated when:

• severe violations are suspected  
• revenue concealment is likely  
• prohibited monetization is detected  

Emergency audits may begin immediately.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — PRE-AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Entities must prepare:

### 4.1 Revenue Documentation
• full revenue logs  
• payment processor statements  
• donation logs  
• subscription logs  
• cosmetic item sales  
• sponsorship agreements  
• advertising revenue reports  

### 4.2 Transaction Data
• transaction IDs  
• timestamps  
• item/service purchased  
• price  
• currency  
• payment platform used  

### 4.3 Monetization Configuration
• monetization settings  
• plugin/mod configuration  
• server configuration  
• permission systems  

### 4.4 Administrative Information
• owner/operator identity  
• approval status  
• staff list  
• contact information  

Failure to prepare documentation is considered obstruction.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — AUDIT PROCESS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The audit process consists of five phases:

### Phase 1 — Notification
The entity receives an audit notice unless the audit is random or emergency.

### Phase 2 — Document Submission
The entity must submit all required documents within 7 days.

### Phase 3 — Technical Inspection
Auditors may inspect:

• server configuration  
• mod code  
• monetization logic  
• payment integrations  
• logs and telemetry  
• sponsorship systems  
• advertising systems  

### Phase 4 — Revenue Verification
Auditors verify:

• total gross revenue  
• revenue share calculations  
• payment processor logs  
• off-platform payments  
• sponsorship disclosures  
• advertising revenue  

### Phase 5 — Compliance Report
The entity receives:

• a pass  
• a list of violations  
• required corrections  
• deadlines  
• follow-up audit date (if needed)  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — AUDITOR ACCESS RIGHTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Auditors may request:

• read-only access to server panels  
• read-only access to mod code  
• access to monetization dashboards  
• access to payment logs  
• access to configuration files  
• access to sponsorship/advertising systems  

Auditors may NOT:

• modify server or mod files  
• access personal user data  
• access payment information of users  

Entities must not:

• block auditor access  
• alter systems during audit  
• delete logs  
• shut down servers to avoid inspection  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 7 — POST-AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Entities must:

• correct all violations before the deadline  
• submit proof of correction  
• maintain compliance going forward  
• cooperate with follow-up audits  

Failure to correct violations escalates penalties.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 8 — SEVERE AUDIT VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following trigger immediate enforcement:

• revenue concealment  
• falsified reports  
• deleted logs  
• unapproved monetization  
• pay-to-win systems  
• crypto/NFT monetization  
• refusal to cooperate  
• obstruction of audit  
• misleading auditors  

Severe violations may result in:

• immediate suspension  
• permanent delisting  
• legal action  
• financial penalties  
• termination of license  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 9 — AUDIT OUTCOMES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Possible outcomes:

### PASS
• entity is compliant  
• no further action required  

### CONDITIONAL PASS
• minor or moderate violations  
• corrections required  
• follow-up audit scheduled  

### FAIL
• severe violations  
• immediate enforcement action  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 10 — AMENDMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

This document may be updated at any time.  
All monetized entities must comply with the latest version.
