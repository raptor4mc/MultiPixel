# Monetization Violation Categories
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 8 and 9 of the Monetization Compliance Rules
and Sections 5, 7, 14, and 15 of the MultiPixel Source-Available License
2.2 — Fortress Edition.

It defines the official violation categories used to classify monetization
misconduct across the MultiPixel ecosystem.

These categories determine:
• enforcement actions
• penalties
• audit escalation
• suspension thresholds
• legal escalation triggers

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW OF VIOLATION LEVELS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Monetization violations are classified into three severity levels:

• **Minor Violations** — low-risk, administrative, or accidental issues  
• **Moderate Violations** — impactful issues that affect compliance integrity  
• **Severe Violations** — high-risk, harmful, or intentionally deceptive actions  

Severity determines the enforcement response.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — MINOR VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Minor violations are unintentional or low-impact issues that do not affect
gameplay fairness, user safety, or revenue integrity.

Examples include:

### 2.1 Reporting Issues
• late revenue reports (first offense)  
• incorrect report formatting  
• missing non-critical fields  
• minor calculation errors  

### 2.2 Cosmetic Labeling Issues
• mislabeled cosmetic items  
• unclear cosmetic descriptions  
• missing “cosmetic-only” tags  

### 2.3 Administrative Issues
• outdated contact information  
• outdated staff list  
• outdated approval documentation  

### 2.4 Technical Issues
• broken cosmetic items  
• non-functional monetization UI  
• temporary payment processor outages  

**Minor violations require correction but do not trigger suspension.**

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — MODERATE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Moderate violations impact compliance integrity, transparency, or user
experience, but do not directly harm gameplay fairness or involve deception.

Examples include:

### 3.1 Reporting & Transparency
• repeated late reports  
• incomplete revenue reports  
• missing transaction logs  
• failure to disclose sponsorships  
• failure to disclose advertising revenue  

### 3.2 Monetization Method Violations
• using unapproved monetization methods  
• enabling cosmetic lootboxes without approval  
• enabling intrusive ads  
• enabling cosmetic subscriptions without disclosure  

### 3.3 Payment Platform Violations
• using unapproved payment processors  
• failing to retain payment logs  
• failing to provide payment confirmations  

### 3.4 Administrative Violations
• ignoring correction deadlines  
• failing to respond to audit requests  
• failing to maintain required documentation  

**Moderate violations may trigger temporary suspension or mandatory audits.**

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — SEVERE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Severe violations are high-risk, harmful, deceptive, or intentionally
non-compliant actions. These violations threaten:

• gameplay fairness  
• user safety  
• financial integrity  
• legal compliance  
• platform trust  

Examples include:

### 4.1 Revenue Concealment
• hiding revenue  
• underreporting revenue  
• deleting payment logs  
• falsifying financial records  
• using off-platform payments to avoid revenue share  

### 4.2 Prohibited Monetization
• pay-to-win systems  
• selling progression, stats, or power  
• selling admin/mod roles  
• selling gameplay advantages  
• gambling or chance-based gameplay rewards  
• crypto/NFT monetization  

### 4.3 Audit Obstruction
• refusing to cooperate with audits  
• deleting logs during an audit  
• altering monetization systems during an audit  
• blocking auditor access  
• providing falsified documents  

### 4.4 Safety & Legal Violations
• monetization targeting minors  
• deceptive or manipulative monetization  
• dark patterns  
• fraudulent sponsorships  
• misleading users about official partnership  

### 4.5 Unauthorized Asset Monetization
• selling MultiPixel assets  
• selling derivative works without approval  
• selling AI-generated recreations of MultiPixel assets  

**Severe violations may result in immediate suspension, delisting, legal
action, or permanent ecosystem bans.**

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — ESCALATION RULES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Violations escalate based on:

• frequency  
• intent  
• severity  
• impact  
• user harm  
• financial harm  
• audit findings  

Examples:

• three minor violations → escalates to moderate  
• two moderate violations → escalates to severe  
• any severe violation → immediate enforcement  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — AUTOMATIC SEVERE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following are *always* severe, regardless of context:

• revenue concealment  
• pay-to-win monetization  
• crypto/NFT monetization  
• gambling systems  
• falsified reports  
• deleted logs  
• refusal to cooperate with audits  
• unauthorized asset sales  
• monetization targeting minors  

These trigger immediate enforcement.
