# Server Violation Categories
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 9, 12, 14, and 15 of the MultiPixel
Source-Available License 2.2 — Fortress Edition.

It defines the official violation categories used to classify Server
misconduct within the MultiPixel ecosystem.

Violations are classified into:
• Minor Violations
• Moderate Violations
• Severe Violations

These categories determine:
• enforcement actions
• penalties
• audit escalation
• suspension thresholds
• license termination triggers

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW OF VIOLATION LEVELS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Minor Violations
Low-impact issues that do not threaten user safety, data integrity, or
ecosystem fairness.

### Moderate Violations
Issues that impact compliance integrity, user experience, or operational
safety, but do not involve deception or severe harm.

### Severe Violations
High-risk, harmful, deceptive, or intentionally non-compliant actions that
threaten user safety, financial integrity, or the MultiPixel ecosystem.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — MINOR VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Minor violations are unintentional or low-impact issues.

Examples include:

### 2.1 Administrative Issues
• outdated staff lists  
• outdated documentation  
• missing rule pages  
• missing safety disclosures  

### 2.2 Logging Issues
• incomplete non-critical logs  
• incorrect log formatting  
• missing timestamps  

### 2.3 Moderation Issues
• inconsistent enforcement of minor rules  
• slow response to low-risk reports  

### 2.4 Technical Issues
• minor configuration errors  
• temporary downtime  
• broken cosmetic features  

Minor violations require correction but do not trigger suspension.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — MODERATE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Moderate violations impact compliance integrity, user experience, or
operational safety.

Examples include:

### 3.1 Logging & Data Issues
• incomplete moderation logs  
• incomplete safety logs  
• incomplete monetization logs  
• insecure log storage  
• unauthorized staff access to logs  

### 3.2 Moderation Issues
• slow response to safety incidents  
• inconsistent enforcement of major rules  
• insufficient chat filtering  
• untrained staff moderating users  

### 3.3 Safety Issues
• failure to prevent harassment  
• failure to prevent hate speech  
• failure to protect minors (non-critical cases)  

### 3.4 Monetization Issues
• unapproved cosmetic monetization  
• intrusive advertising  
• missing revenue disclosures  

### 3.5 Operational Issues
• repeated downtime  
• unapproved plugins (non-malicious)  
• misconfigured security settings  

Moderate violations may trigger temporary suspension or mandatory audits.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — SEVERE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Severe violations are high-risk, harmful, deceptive, or intentionally
non-compliant actions.

Examples include:

### 4.1 Safety & User Protection
• allowing hate speech  
• allowing extremist content  
• allowing targeted harassment  
• allowing sexual content involving minors  
• ignoring safety reports  
• failing to protect minors  
• enabling predatory behavior  

### 4.2 Moderation Abuse
• selling moderator/admin roles  
• staff abusing power  
• unlogged moderation actions  
• retaliation against reporters  
• deleting moderation logs  

### 4.3 Data & Privacy Violations
• collecting personal data  
• collecting sensitive data  
• selling or sharing user data  
• tracking users across servers  
• fingerprinting users  
• deleting logs during audits  

### 4.4 Monetization Violations
• pay-to-win systems  
• selling progression, stats, or power  
• gambling systems  
• crypto/NFT monetization  
• revenue concealment  
• deleted payment logs  
• unapproved monetization on unapproved servers  

### 4.5 Operational & Security Violations
• running malicious plugins  
• modifying MultiPixel client code  
• bypassing security systems  
• impersonating official MultiPixel servers  

Any severe violation may result in immediate enforcement.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — ESCALATION RULES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Violations escalate based on:

• frequency  
• severity  
• intent  
• user harm  
• financial harm  
• audit findings  

Escalation examples:

• 3 minor violations → escalates to moderate  
• 2 moderate violations → escalates to severe  
• any severe violation → immediate enforcement  
• repeated moderate violations → severe  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — AUTOMATIC SEVERE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following ALWAYS result in severe classification:

• pay-to-win monetization  
• selling staff roles  
• staff abusing power  
• sexual content involving minors  
• extremist content  
• hate speech  
• revenue concealment  
• deleted logs  
• refusal to cooperate with audits  
• impersonating official MultiPixel services  
• collecting personal or sensitive data  
• crypto/NFT monetization  
• gambling systems  

These violations bypass all lower tiers.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 7 — RELATIONSHIP TO PENALTIES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Violation categories determine penalties defined in:

• Server_Penalty_Tiers.md  

Minor → Tier 0–1  
Moderate → Tier 1–2  
Severe → Tier 2–3
