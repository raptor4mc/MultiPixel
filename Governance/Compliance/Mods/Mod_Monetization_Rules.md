# Mod Monetization Rules
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 7, 10, 12, and 14 of the MultiPixel
Source-Available License 2.2 — Fortress Edition.

It defines the allowed and prohibited monetization methods for Mods within
the MultiPixel ecosystem. ALL monetized Mods require approval.

No Mod may generate revenue without explicit approval.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — GENERAL MONETIZATION PRINCIPLES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Mod monetization must follow these principles:

• fairness  
• transparency  
• user protection  
• no pay-to-win  
• no gambling  
• no crypto/NFT systems  
• no deceptive practices  
• no hidden monetization  
• no data-selling  
• no dark patterns  

Monetization must NEVER impact gameplay fairness or user safety.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — ALLOWED MONETIZATION METHODS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following monetization methods are allowed **with approval**:

### 2.1 Cosmetic Sales
• skins  
• textures  
• models  
• animations  
• sound packs  
• cosmetic-only items  

Cosmetics must not affect gameplay.

### 2.2 Content Packs
• new blocks  
• new items  
• new mobs  
• new biomes  
• new structures  
• new worldgen features  

Content packs must not introduce pay-to-win mechanics.

### 2.3 Expansions
• large content expansions  
• new gameplay systems (non-competitive)  
• new story content  
• new adventure content  

### 2.4 Donations
• voluntary donations  
• optional supporter perks (cosmetic only)  

### 2.5 Subscriptions (Cosmetic or Content Access Only)
• cosmetic subscription bundles  
• content access subscriptions  

Subscriptions must NOT:

• provide power  
• provide progression  
• provide competitive advantage  

### 2.6 Sponsorships (With Disclosure)
• brand partnerships  
• sponsored cosmetic items  
• sponsored content packs  

Sponsorships must be disclosed clearly.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — PROHIBITED MONETIZATION METHODS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following monetization methods are **strictly prohibited**:

### 3.1 Pay-to-Win Systems
• selling weapons  
• selling armor  
• selling stat boosts  
• selling progression  
• selling power  
• selling competitive advantages  

### 3.2 Gambling Systems
• loot boxes  
• chance-based rewards  
• slot-machine mechanics  
• roulette mechanics  
• gacha systems  

### 3.3 Crypto/NFT Monetization
• NFTs  
• blockchain items  
• crypto tokens  
• crypto-based progression  
• crypto-based trading  

### 3.4 Data Monetization
• selling user data  
• selling analytics  
• selling behavioral data  
• selling tracking data  

### 3.5 Dark Patterns
• disguised purchases  
• forced purchases  
• manipulative UI  
• misleading pricing  

### 3.6 Unauthorized External Payments
• unapproved payment platforms  
• unapproved marketplaces  
• off-platform purchases that bypass revenue share  

### 3.7 Monetization Without Approval
Any monetization without approval is a severe violation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — REVENUE SHARING
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

All monetized Mods must follow the official revenue share:

### 4.1 Standard Revenue Share
• **30% MultiPixel Platform Fee**  
• **70% Developer Share**

This applies to:

• cosmetic sales  
• content packs  
• expansions  
• subscriptions  
• sponsorships (if revenue is involved)  

### 4.2 Donation Revenue Share
• **0% Platform Fee** for voluntary donations  
• **30% Platform Fee** if donations unlock cosmetic perks  

### 4.3 Server-Integrated Monetization
If a Mod enables monetization on a server:

• server revenue share applies  
• mod revenue share applies  
• both systems must be approved  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

All monetized Mods must disclose:

• all monetization methods  
• all prices  
• all subscription terms  
• all sponsorships  
• all data collection  
• all external services used  

Disclosures must be:

• clear  
• accurate  
• visible  
• honest  

Hidden monetization is a severe violation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — TRANSACTION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

All transactions must:

• use approved payment platforms  
• be logged accurately  
• be stored securely  
• be auditable  
• follow data protection rules  

Mods must NOT:

• store payment data  
• process payments directly  
• use insecure payment methods  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 7 — AUDIT REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Monetized Mods must provide:

• transaction logs  
• revenue reports  
• payment platform logs  
• monetization configuration  
• sponsorship agreements (if applicable)  

Refusal to provide monetization data is a severe violation.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 8 — ENFORCEMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Violations of these rules may result in:

• removal from the Mod Marketplace  
• suspension of monetization  
• permanent monetization bans  
• delisting  
• legal action  
• license termination  

See:
• Mod_Violation_Categories.md  
• Mod_Penalty_Tiers.md  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 9 — AMENDMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

This document may be updated at any time.  
All Mods must comply with the latest version.
