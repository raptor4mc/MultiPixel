# Mod Violation Categories
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 7, 10, 12, and 14 of the MultiPixel
Source-Available License 2.2 — Fortress Edition.

It defines the official violation categories for Mods within the MultiPixel
ecosystem. These categories determine enforcement actions, penalties, and
audit requirements.

Violations are classified into three tiers:
• Minor Violations  
• Major Violations  
• Severe Violations  

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — MINOR VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Minor Violations are low-impact issues that do not threaten user safety,
ecosystem integrity, or platform security. They are typically caused by
oversight, outdated content, or technical mistakes.

### 1.1 Examples of Minor Violations
• incorrect metadata  
• missing tags  
• inaccurate descriptions  
• outdated screenshots  
• minor compatibility issues  
• small performance inefficiencies  
• missing content warnings  
• incomplete documentation  
• minor UI bugs  
• non-harmful scripting errors  

### 1.2 Characteristics
• unintentional  
• low risk  
• easy to fix  
• no user harm  
• no security impact  

### 1.3 Required Actions
• fix required within a reasonable timeframe  
• update submission materials  
• resubmit if necessary  

Repeated Minor Violations may escalate to Major Violations.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — MAJOR VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Major Violations are significant issues that impact gameplay fairness,
stability, transparency, or compliance. They may harm the ecosystem or
users but do not involve malicious intent.

### 2.1 Examples of Major Violations
• hidden monetization  
• unapproved monetization  
• unapproved data collection  
• misleading descriptions  
• significant performance degradation  
• unbalanced gameplay systems  
• unfair competitive advantages  
• bypassing minor safety systems  
• unauthorized external communication  
• failure to disclose sponsorships  
• failure to disclose telemetry  
• distributing updates without approval  

### 2.2 Characteristics
• medium risk  
• may harm user experience  
• may impact fairness  
• may violate transparency rules  
• may degrade ecosystem integrity  

### 2.3 Required Actions
• immediate fix required  
• temporary suspension possible  
• full review of updated version  
• audit may be triggered  

Repeated Major Violations may escalate to Severe Violations.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — SEVERE VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Severe Violations are the highest category. These violations threaten user
safety, platform security, legal compliance, or the integrity of the entire
ecosystem. They often involve malicious intent or extremely dangerous
content.

### 3.1 Examples of Severe Violations
• malware  
• backdoors  
• unauthorized telemetry  
• data harvesting  
• personal data collection  
• extremist content  
• hate speech  
• sexual content involving minors  
• gambling systems  
• crypto/NFT monetization  
• pay-to-win systems  
• bypassing engine security  
• code obfuscation used to hide harmful behavior  
• impersonating official MultiPixel content  
• distributing Mods without approval  
• attempting to bypass the approval system  
• attempting to bypass audits  
• tampering with platform infrastructure  
• exploiting vulnerabilities intentionally  

### 3.2 Characteristics
• high risk  
• harmful or malicious  
• threatens user safety  
• threatens platform integrity  
• violates legal or ethical standards  

### 3.3 Required Actions
• immediate removal  
• permanent ban possible  
• monetization revoked  
• legal action possible  
• license termination possible  
• full audit required  

Severe Violations are treated with zero tolerance.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 4 — ESCALATION RULES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Violations may escalate based on:

• repeated offenses  
• refusal to fix issues  
• attempts to hide violations  
• user harm  
• security impact  
• ecosystem impact  

Escalation may move a violation to a higher category.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 5 — DEVELOPER RESPONSIBILITY
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Developers are responsible for:

• understanding all rules  
• maintaining compliance  
• updating Mods promptly  
• disclosing all changes  
• cooperating with audits  

Failure to cooperate may escalate violations.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — ENFORCEMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Penalties for violations are defined in:

• Mod_Penalty_Tiers.md  

Enforcement actions may include:

• warnings  
• suspensions  
• delisting  
• monetization bans  
• permanent bans  
• legal action  
• license termination

All Mods must comply with the latest version.
