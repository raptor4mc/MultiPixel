# Mods Compliance Suite
### MultiPixel Governance & Enforcement — Mods Division  
Version 1.0 — Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

The **Mods Compliance Suite** defines the complete governance, safety,
monetization, and enforcement framework for all Mods within the MultiPixel
ecosystem.

ALL Mods must:
• be submitted for approval  
• comply with all rules in this directory  
• follow the MultiPixel Source-Available License 2.2 — Fortress Edition  
• maintain user safety, fairness, and transparency  
• cooperate with audits  

This directory contains the official rulebooks that govern how Mods are
created, reviewed, distributed, monetized, and enforced.

---

## 📁 Included Documents

### **1. Mod_Compliance_Rules.md**
The backbone of the entire Mods governance system.  
Defines the global rules all Mods must follow, including technical,
security, safety, data, and operational requirements.

### **2. Mod_Approval_Requirements.md**
Explains the approval process for Mods.  
ALL Mods require approval before distribution.  
Defines review tiers, submission requirements, and automatic denial
conditions.

### **3. Mod_Monetization_Rules.md**
Defines allowed and prohibited monetization methods.  
Covers cosmetic sales, content packs, expansions, subscriptions,
sponsorships, donations, and the official revenue share (70/30).  
Strictly prohibits pay-to-win, gambling, crypto/NFTs, and hidden
monetization.

### **4. Mod_Safety_Standards.md**
Defines mandatory safety rules for Mods.  
Covers harmful content, harassment systems, predatory mechanics, mature
themes, political content, and multiplayer safety.

### **5. Mod_Data_Requirements.md**
Defines what data Mods may collect, how it must be stored, and what is
prohibited.  
Strictly bans personal data, sensitive data, fingerprinting, unauthorized
telemetry, and hidden data transmission.

### **6. Mod_Content_Restrictions.md**
Defines allowed, restricted, and prohibited content.  
Covers illegal content, extremist content, unsafe gameplay, mature themes,
political content, and content warnings.

### **7. Mod_Violation_Categories.md**
Defines the three violation tiers:
• Minor Violations  
• Major Violations  
• Severe Violations  

Each tier includes examples and escalation rules.

### **8. Mod_Penalty_Tiers.md**
Defines the penalties for each violation tier.  
Covers warnings, suspensions, delisting, monetization bans, permanent
developer bans, and legal action.

### **9. Mod_Audit_Procedure.md**
Defines how audits work, when they occur, what developers must provide, and
what happens if they refuse.  
Covers routine audits, triggered audits, and full investigative audits.

## 🎯 Purpose of This Suite

The Mods Compliance Suite ensures that:

• users are protected  
• the ecosystem remains safe and fair  
• Mods cannot hide malware or harmful behavior  
• monetization is transparent and ethical  
• data collection is controlled and secure  
• content stays within legal and ethical boundaries  
• developers follow consistent rules  
• enforcement is predictable and documented  

This suite is designed to operate at a **professional, platform-level
standard**, similar to major ecosystems such as Roblox, Minecraft
Marketplace, and Unreal Engine.
