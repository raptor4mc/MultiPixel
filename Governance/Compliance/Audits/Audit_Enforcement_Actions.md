
# Audit_Enforcement_Actions.md
Version 2.0 — Deep‑Dive Edition
Relimintor (Raptor4 / MultiPixel)

This document expands Sections 7, 10, 14, and 15 of the MultiPixel Source‑Available License 2.2 — Fortress Edition. It defines the authoritative enforcement actions applied when any individual, operator, developer, or entity violates audit‑related obligations. This document governs enforcement for all audit types defined in Audit_Types_and_Triggers.md and all audit procedures defined in Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md. It does not define methodology, evidence requirements, or escalation logic; those are delegated to other audit files.

This document applies to:
• Mods
• Servers
• Tools
• Integrations
• APIs
• Monetized systems
• Third‑party services
• Any system subject to audit under MultiPixel governance

This document establishes:
• enforcement authority
• enforcement scope
• violation categories
• penalty tiers
• enforcement actions
• documentation requirements
• operator obligations
• auditor obligations
• compliance expectations

This document does NOT define:
• audit methodology (see Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md)
• evidence requirements (see Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md)
• documentation standards (see Audit_Documentation_Standards.md)
• escalation rules (see Audit_Escalation_Policy.md)
• audit triggers (see Audit_Types_and_Triggers.md)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 1 — PURPOSE OF AUDIT ENFORCEMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The purpose of this document is to:
• ensure that audit violations result in consistent, predictable, and enforceable consequences
• ensure that operators and developers comply with audit requirements
• ensure that evidence is preserved, protected, and produced when required
• ensure that audit findings lead to corrective action
• ensure that MultiPixel can enforce the MultiPixel License and all governance documents
• ensure that the audit system maintains integrity, authority, and legal defensibility

Audit enforcement is mandatory. Failure to comply with audit obligations is treated as a violation of the MultiPixel License.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Audit Violation  
Any action or omission that interferes with, obstructs, delays, or compromises an audit.

2.2 Enforcement Action  
Any penalty, restriction, suspension, or corrective measure imposed as a result of an audit violation.

2.3 Operator  
Any individual or entity responsible for a system under audit.

2.4 Auditor  
Any MultiPixel‑authorized individual conducting an audit.

2.5 Evidence  
Any material required to support audit findings. See Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md.

2.6 Remediation  
Actions required to correct violations identified during an audit.

2.7 Penalty Tier  
A classification of violation severity used to determine enforcement actions.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 3 — ENFORCEMENT AUTHORITY
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 MultiPixel Enforcement Authority  
MultiPixel retains full authority to impose enforcement actions for any audit violation.

3.2 Auditor Authority  
Auditors may:
• request evidence
• request documentation
• request access to systems
• request operator statements
• initiate enforcement recommendations

3.3 Compliance Division Authority  
The Compliance Division may:
• approve enforcement actions
• escalate enforcement actions
• coordinate with legal teams
• coordinate with security teams
• coordinate with audit custodians

3.4 Legal Authority  
MultiPixel may pursue legal action for severe or critical violations.

SECTION 5 — ENFORCEMENT PRINCIPLES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Consistency  
Enforcement actions must be applied consistently across all systems and operators.

5.2 Proportionality  
Penalties must match the severity of the violation.

5.3 Evidence‑Based  
Enforcement must be supported by evidence documented according to Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md.

5.4 Documentation  
All enforcement actions must be documented according to Audit_Documentation_Standards.md.

5.5 Transparency  
Operators must be informed of enforcement actions unless doing so compromises an investigation.

5.6 Non‑Negotiability  
Audit enforcement is not subject to negotiation, appeal, or delay unless explicitly permitted by MultiPixel.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 6 — VIOLATION CATEGORIES (PENALTY TIERS)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Minor Audit Violations  
Definition: Low‑impact violations that do not compromise audit integrity but violate procedural requirements.  
Examples include:
• late submission of non‑critical documentation
• minor formatting errors
• incomplete metadata
• minor delays in communication

6.2 Major Audit Violations  
Definition: Violations that hinder audit progress or compromise partial audit integrity.  
Examples include:
• failure to provide required evidence on time
• incomplete evidence bundles
• repeated minor violations
• failure to follow audit procedures

6.3 Severe Audit Violations  
Definition: Violations that significantly compromise audit integrity or obstruct auditors.  
Examples include:
• deletion of required evidence
• tampering with logs
• refusal to provide access
• falsified documentation

6.4 Critical Audit Violations  
Definition: Violations that destroy audit integrity, compromise security, or violate the MultiPixel License.  
Examples include:
• intentional destruction of evidence
• intentional falsification of audit materials
• deliberate obstruction of auditors
• coordinated attempts to evade audits
• unauthorized data manipulation

SECTION 8 — ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS FOR MINOR AUDIT VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

8.1 Definition  
Minor violations are low‑impact procedural failures that do not compromise audit integrity but violate required audit processes.

8.2 Applicable When  
• documentation is late but submitted within grace period  
• metadata is incomplete but correctable  
• formatting errors impede readability  
• communication delays occur without impact on audit timeline  
• minor deviations from Audit_Documentation_Standards.md  
• minor deviations from Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md

8.3 Enforcement Actions  
Minor violations may result in:  
• written warning  
• mandatory correction of documentation  
• mandatory resubmission  
• extension of audit scope  
• requirement for operator training  
• increased monitoring in future audits  
• minor administrative penalties

8.4 Documentation Requirements  
All minor enforcement actions must be documented according to Audit_Documentation_Standards.md and must reference:  
• violation category  
• evidence ID  
• policy reference  
• corrective action  
• timeline

8.5 Timelines  
Minor violations must be corrected within 7 days unless otherwise specified in Audit_Escalation_Policy.md.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 9 — ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS FOR MAJOR AUDIT VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

9.1 Definition  
Major violations hinder audit progress or compromise partial audit integrity.

9.2 Applicable When  
• required evidence is not provided on time  
• evidence bundles are incomplete  
• repeated minor violations occur  
• operator fails to follow audit procedures  
• operator fails to comply with Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md  
• operator fails to comply with Audit_Types_and_Triggers.md  
• operator fails to comply with Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md

9.3 Enforcement Actions  
Major violations may result in:  
• mandatory follow‑up audit  
• temporary suspension of system privileges  
• temporary suspension of monetization (if applicable)  
• mandatory operator compliance training  
• mandatory documentation overhaul  
• increased audit frequency  
• administrative penalties  
• temporary restrictions on system updates or releases

9.4 Documentation Requirements  
Major enforcement actions must include:  
• violation description  
• evidence reference  
• policy reference  
• enforcement action  
• remediation requirement  
• follow‑up audit requirement  
• timeline

9.5 Timelines  
Major violations must be corrected within 72 hours unless otherwise specified in Audit_Escalation_Policy.md.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 10 — ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS FOR SEVERE AUDIT VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

10.1 Definition  
Severe violations significantly compromise audit integrity or obstruct auditors.

10.2 Applicable When  
• evidence is deleted  
• logs are tampered with  
• access is refused  
• documentation is falsified  
• operator obstructs auditors  
• operator violates Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md in a way that compromises audit integrity  
• operator violates Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md in a way that obstructs audit execution

10.3 Enforcement Actions  
Severe violations may result in:  
• immediate suspension of system privileges  
• immediate suspension of monetization (if applicable)  
• forced system freeze pending investigation  
• mandatory forensic audit  
• mandatory full evidence reconstruction  
• administrative penalties  
• long‑term monitoring  
• temporary removal from MultiPixel ecosystem  
• temporary revocation of development or operator status

10.4 Documentation Requirements  
Severe enforcement actions must include:  
• violation category  
• detailed description  
• evidence reference  
• policy reference  
• enforcement action  
• remediation requirement  
• forensic audit requirement  
• timeline  
• escalation reference (Audit_Escalation_Policy.md)

10.5 Timelines  
Severe violations must be addressed within 24 hours unless otherwise specified in Audit_Escalation_Policy.md.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 11 — ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS FOR CRITICAL AUDIT VIOLATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

11.1 Definition  
Critical violations destroy audit integrity, compromise security, or violate the MultiPixel License.

11.2 Applicable When  
• evidence is intentionally destroyed  
• documentation is intentionally falsified  
• auditors are deliberately obstructed  
• coordinated attempts to evade audits occur  
• unauthorized data manipulation occurs  
• operator attempts to bypass audit triggers defined in Audit_Types_and_Triggers.md  
• operator attempts to bypass evidence requirements defined in Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md

11.3 Enforcement Actions  
Critical violations may result in:  
• immediate system shutdown  
• immediate revocation of system privileges  
• permanent removal from MultiPixel ecosystem  
• permanent revocation of monetization (if applicable)  
• permanent revocation of development or operator status  
• mandatory legal escalation  
• mandatory forensic audit  
• mandatory full data reconstruction  
• administrative penalties  
• long‑term compliance monitoring

11.4 Documentation Requirements  
Critical enforcement actions must include:  
• violation category  
• detailed description  
• evidence reference  
• policy reference  
• enforcement action  
• remediation requirement  
• forensic audit requirement  
• legal escalation requirement  
• timeline  
• cross‑file references to Audit_Escalation_Policy.md and Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md

11.5 Timelines  
Critical violations must be addressed immediately.

SECTION 12 — EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS FOR ENFORCEMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

12.1 Evidence Standards  
All evidence used to justify enforcement actions must comply with Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md.

12.2 Evidence Types  
Evidence may include:  
• logs  
• screenshots  
• storage snapshots  
• configuration files  
• operator statements  
• auditor notes  
• network traces  
• metadata blocks  
• documentation excerpts

12.3 Evidence Integrity  
Evidence must be:  
• hashed  
• timestamped  
• classified  
• stored securely  
• referenced by ID  
• preserved until retention period expires

12.4 Evidence Bundles  
Enforcement evidence must be grouped into bundles and referenced by bundle ID.

12.5 Evidence Documentation  
All evidence must be documented according to Audit_Documentation_Standards.md.

SECTION 14 — ENFORCEMENT WORKFLOW
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

14.1 Workflow Overview  
All enforcement actions must follow a structured workflow to ensure consistency, fairness, and compliance with MultiPixel governance. The workflow consists of:  
1. Violation Identification  
2. Evidence Collection  
3. Violation Classification  
4. Enforcement Determination  
5. Operator Notification  
6. Remediation Assignment  
7. Follow‑Up Audit  
8. Closure and Archival

14.2 Violation Identification  
Violations may be identified by:  
• auditors  
• automated systems  
• compliance division  
• security division  
• cross‑audit triggers defined in Audit_Types_and_Triggers.md

14.3 Evidence Collection  
Evidence must be collected and documented according to Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md. Evidence must be hashed, timestamped, classified, and stored securely.

14.4 Violation Classification  
Violations must be classified into Minor, Major, Severe, or Critical categories according to Sections 6–11 of this document.

14.5 Enforcement Determination  
Enforcement actions must be selected based on:  
• violation category  
• evidence strength  
• operator history  
• audit context  
• cross‑file requirements defined in Audit_Escalation_Policy.md

14.6 Operator Notification  
Operators must be notified of enforcement actions unless notification compromises an investigation. Notification must include:  
• violation description  
• evidence reference  
• enforcement action  
• remediation requirement  
• timeline  
• follow‑up audit requirement

14.7 Remediation Assignment  
Remediation requirements must be assigned according to Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md and must include:  
• required actions  
• deadlines  
• verification method  
• evidence submission requirements  
• follow‑up audit requirements

14.8 Follow‑Up Audit  
Follow‑up audits must be conducted to verify remediation. Follow‑up audits must follow the procedures defined in Audit_Methodology_and_Standards.md.

14.9 Closure and Archival  
Once remediation is verified, enforcement actions must be closed and archived according to Data_Retention_and_Deletion.md and Data_Storage_Requirements.md.

SECTION 15 — ENFORCEMENT TIMELINES
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

15.1 Timeline Overview  
Timelines ensure that enforcement actions are executed promptly and consistently. Timelines must follow Audit_Escalation_Policy.md unless otherwise specified.

15.2 Minor Violations  
• remediation deadline: 7 days  
• follow‑up audit: within 14 days  
• documentation update: immediate

15.3 Major Violations  
• remediation deadline: 72 hours  
• follow‑up audit: within 7 days  
• documentation update: immediate

15.4 Severe Violations  
• remediation deadline: 24 hours  
• forensic audit: immediate  
• follow‑up audit: within 72 hours  
• documentation update: immediate

15.5 Critical Violations  
• remediation deadline: immediate  
• forensic audit: immediate  
• legal escalation: immediate  
• system suspension: immediate  
• documentation update: immediate

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 16 — REMEDIATION ENFORCEMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

16.1 Remediation Requirements  
Remediation must address:  
• root cause  
• affected systems  
• affected data  
• required changes  
• verification method  
• evidence submission  
• follow‑up audit requirements

16.2 Remediation Evidence  
Operators must provide evidence demonstrating that remediation is complete. Evidence must comply with Audit_Evidence_Requirements.md.

16.3 Remediation Verification  
Verification must be performed by auditors and documented according to Audit_Documentation_Standards.md.

16.4 Remediation Failure  
Failure to complete remediation results in:  
• escalation to next penalty tier  
• mandatory forensic audit  
• temporary suspension of system privileges  
• administrative penalties  
• potential legal escalation

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 17 — ESCALATION ENFORCEMENT
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

17.1 Escalation Overview  
Escalation occurs when:  
• violations worsen  
• remediation fails  
• evidence is compromised  
• operators obstruct auditors  
• timelines are missed  
• repeated violations occur

17.2 Escalation Levels  
Escalation must follow the structure defined in Audit_Escalation_Policy.md.

17.3 Automatic Escalation  
Automatic escalation occurs when:  
• evidence is deleted  
• logs are tampered with  
• access is refused  
• documentation is falsified  
• critical violations occur

17.4 Escalation Actions  
Escalation may result in:  
• system suspension  
• monetization suspension (if applicable)  
• forensic audit  
• legal escalation  
• permanent removal from MultiPixel ecosystem
